> **The One Thing Most People Miss:** <br/><br/>
> ***Specificity about your L1.***<br/>
> Your accent is predictable based on your first language. A Spanish speaker
> drops
> different sounds than a Mandarin speaker or a Hindi speaker. The more you tell
> the AI about your background, the more targeted the feedback.

## Why Your Background Matters First

Telugu, Kannada, and Hindi all share traits that directly cause your weak spots:

| #  | Your L1 Pattern                                 | English Problem It Creates                        |
|----|-------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| 1. | Telugu/Kannada are syllable-timed languages     | Flat monotone — every syllable gets equal weight  |
| 2. | No th sound exists in any Indian language       | Substituting d, t, or z for th                    |
| 3. | Telugu v/w are near-identical sounds            | Mixing wine and vine, wet and vet                 |
| 4. | Sentence structure is SOV (Subject-Object-Verb) | Grammar inversions, verbose phrasing              |
| 5. | Rich descriptive tradition in Telugu            | Over-explaining instead of choosing precise words |

## Lexical precision

**Word choice** — when two words seem identical, knowing the exact right word
for the exact right context.

| Confusing Pair   | You're unsure which to use                      |
|------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| which vs that"   | The book that I read" vs "The book which I read |
| "begin vs start" | Let's start" vs "Let's begin                    |
| "big vs large"   | A big problem" vs "A large problem              |
| "speak vs talk"  | Can I talk to you?" vs " Can I speak to you?    |
| "make vs do"     | Make a decision" vs "Do a decision"             |

## #1 Priority: Stress-Timing

This is the single biggest thing separating you from sounding native.
English is stress-timed — meaning stressed syllables hit at regular intervals,
and unstressed syllables compress between them.

> **Example:**<br/>
*You likely say:* <br/>
> `I am go-ing to the mar-ket (every syllable equal)`<br/>
*Native sounds like:* <br/>
> `I'm GOing to the MARket (compressed, bouncy)`

## Structure: 4 Modules, Sequential

| #  | Module               | Focus                                    | Why First                                          |
|----|----------------------|------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| 1. | Stress & Rhythm      | Word stress, sentence beats              | Biggest gap. Fixes monotone immediately            |
| 2. | Sound Repair         | th / v / w mouth mechanics               | Isolated drill before using in sentences           |  
| 3. | Grammar Patterns     | SOV → SVO rewiring, tense accuracy       | Builds correct sentence instinct                   |  
| 4. | Vocabulary Precision | Replace verbose phrases with exact words | Only works after rhythm + grammar are stable       |  
| 5. | Lexical precision    | Word Choice & Confusing Pairs            | which/that, begin/start, make/do, speak/talk, etc. |

## Daily Session Format (45–60 mins)

| Time    | Activity                                        |
|---------|-------------------------------------------------|
| 10 mins | Concept — Teach one rule with examples          |
| 20 mins | Script — mark what to notice                    |
| 15 mins | Drill — Targeted repetition of the hardest part |
| 10 mins | Real life — Apply it in daily usage             |

## How Scripts Work

- Write a short script (3–8 sentences)
- Mark it with **STRESS CAPS**, **slash** marks for pauses /
- I read it aloud
- I type back what felt hard
- You explain and we drill that specific part

**One Ground Rule:**

- I tell you after every script what felt unnatural in my mouth. That's my job.
- Your job is to explain why and fix it.
